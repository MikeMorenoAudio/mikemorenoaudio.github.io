About

Miguel Moreno is a freelance sound designer, audio programmer, and one half of the experimental music duo DEHUS.

He studied music production and engineering at the Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education in Monterrey, México (2011-2016), and got his initial audio engineering experiences by volunteering at BHD Studios and Arturia (2015-2016). There, he was involved in the sound editing for several Latin-American films, the beta testing for Arturia’s V Collection 5, and the development of a drum sampler prototype for the BeatStep Pro.

After graduating as an audio engineer, Miguel became the Lead Audio Programmer for Moon Echo Audio, where he is currently developing a fully synthesized drum machine.

In 2018, he worked as a sound designer and programmer for interactive art installations at Monterrey’s Museum of Contemporary Art (MARCO): Kessler I and Neon SciFi: Neuro.

Miguel’s current personal project is creating a library of synthesized instruments and sounds for Pure Data vanilla. These range from electric pianos and acoustic drums to waterfalls and dog barks.
